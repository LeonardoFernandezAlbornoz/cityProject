Monuments

In this article we will talk about some of the most important monuments in the city of beijing

In Beijing, there are some of the most beautiful monuments of antiquity and various historical relics related
to China’s imperial past.

The Forbidden City

The Forbidden City is the largest ancient monuments in Beijing. It was built-in 1406. It covers an area of
720,000 square meters with a construction area of about 150,000 square meters. More than 70 palaces
and over 9,000 rooms are located in the 961-meter-long, 753-meter-wide and 10-meter-high rectangular
courtyard. The important palaces are on the axis of the courtyard. A row of mystical animal statuettes
features along the ridgeline of halls. Red walls and yellow tiles symbolize the supreme power of the imperial
family. The Forbidden City embodies the essence of traditional Chinese architecture.

Tian’anmen Square

The design of the National Emblem of the People’s Republic of China shows Tian’anmen. Tian’anmen Square
is at the center of Beijing. It is spread across an area of 440,000 square meters can host a grand gathering
of one million people. It is the largest city square and one of the important monuments in Beijing. In 1949,
Chairman Mao proclaimed the foundation of the People’s Republic of China from the Tian’anmen rostrum.
The Mausoleum of Mao Zedong is at the south of the square; where Chairman Mao’s body lies in a crystal
coffin. Tian’anmen has been popular for various important events in Chinese history. It, therefore, has a
high status in the hearts of many Chinese people.

Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China is one of the most visited sites and famous monuments in Beijing in the country of
China. The section that is usually visited by most tourists is the first part, which has a completely rebuilt
wall and is closest to the center of Beijing.

The Simatai section

This stretch has a unique landscape for two reasons: One, is the lack of people, since its conditions and
remoteness are not very visited, and the other reason is its status: The wall remains how it was, with no
reconstructions or additions. It is highly recommended for those who want to see things as they were and
do not mind walking in-between rubble.
